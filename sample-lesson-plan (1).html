2Learning goals

  • Challenge assumptions about who trades careers are "for"

4Pause points — what to do

The video stops automatically at each pause point. Run it one of two ways:

⏸ Pause point 1 · after the welder

"Which of these three surprised you — and why?"

📽️ Projecting to the class

Quick show of hands: who'd never heard of one of these? Then 60-second turn-and-talk with a neighbour. Call on two pairs.

💻 Students on devices

Students tap an answer to "Had you heard of these trades before?" — anonymous. The class result appears on screen; you react to the split.

⏸ Pause point 2 · after the millwright

"Which of these could you see yourself trying — and what would you need to find out?"

📽️ Projecting to the class

Ask for one "I'd try…" from three volunteers, and one question each has about that path. Note them on the board.

💻 Students on devices

Students tap "Could you see yourself doing this?" (Yes / Maybe / Not for me) — anonymous. This feeds the awareness-lift comparison with pause point 1.

No devices? Both pause points work perfectly as spoken discussion. The device version only adds anonymous class-level data — it's never required.

6Offline reflection sheet

Print one per student, or project and have them answer in a notebook. Works with zero devices.

Trades That Build Ontario · name: ___________________
1. Name two trades from the clips you didn't know existed (or knew little about).
2. Pick the one that interests you most. What about it appeals to you?
3. What's one thing you'd need to find out to pursue it? (training, cost, where to start)
4. Before today, did trades feel like an option for someone like you? Has that changed? Why or why not?
